7 Top Ways to Attract and Keep Talented Millennials

  • June 29, 2015
  • Joe Grande
  •

I’m a 27 Year old business professional. My name is Joe Grande and I wanted to share with you some insight from a millennial perspective on why our generation is different and how companies can use this difference to their advantage for attracting and keeping the best young talent.

1 – Incredibly Curious Generation

Why, Why, Why, maybe we ask too many questions as to why our company does things a certain way, but this is what makes us such a powerful generation. We are the type of employees who will pull back the blinds a bit to see what happens downstream, and get an insight on why things are done the way they are. Bottom line is we are interested in knowing more than what our job description specifies, we are curious to find out how things work and will find ways to understand them better.

2 – We try to work smarter, not harder

We’re not actually lazy! We just hate to see our time wasted on things that are not important or are blatant redundancies. If I was ever asked to store a document in more than one location, I’d find out why this was standard protocol and work diligently to simplify it.

Growing up using apps that consolidate all of our redundancies has turned us into smarter workers, not lazier employees! We expect to perform a process in the smartest way possible in order to truly be happy performing that process.

3 – Quick Learners, we are able to find information faster than ever

Point me to a google search and I can learn in depth knowledge about a topic, best practice, or world issue within a matter of minutes. This isn’t a new concept, we all use the internet to search for things and that’s true across pretty well all working generations. However our generation has become so good at learning by using the internet the limitation on our abilities is almost non-existent.

Now alternatively, point me at an internal filing cabinet and ask me to review company procedures and we might fall asleep before we find the right document. Or give us access to training documents strewn across the company internal network and I might just skip reading the ones I couldn’t find.

4 – We don’t like “Paying our dues”

Tell us we need to sit in a chair for 10 years and pay our dues to advance is not what we want to hear. We want to contribute to the overall success of the company and contribute in a way where we can see the difference we are making. We don’t look at our peers as competition, we look at them as our friends, people we want to advance with us, with the company. Our willingness to work with other people to succeed as a group sets millennials apart. Things like group messaging and other tech tools we use on a daily basis for social reasons actually have trained us to share more, and help others succeed with us.

5 – We want more than ever to make a change

I get excited when I’m on a new project to change the way an old process is done, that’s because I get to imagine what is possible. Brainstorming sessions are like our “Insert Addiction Here” we are totally excited to share our knowledge and come up with ideas that haven’t been thought of before. We will contribute to any improvement project that allows us to either a. Learn or b. Share our Knowledge, so pretty well, any project!

6 – Hungry to expand our Skill Set

Millennials get excited when we get the opportunity to learn something new. If that means learning how accounting processes receivables then so be it!

7 – Show us the big picture!

We want to know what it’s all for. The company should actively try to maintain a culture within their organization of sharing successes of the company and other avenues they are pursuing to expand the business. This doesn’t just apply to sales/marketing/business development, we also mean internally within the operational departments. What are some of the changes the finance team made last month? What are they working on next month? Can I be of assistance to any of those initiatives?

We are keen to read about how the company is innovating, and we will spend time reading about it to find out what it’s all about. You can be sure that the brain is not turned on 100% of the work day doing actual work, but in downtime I might read up on how the warehouse team implemented a barcode software to automate their picking and packing processes. Yes, even things that are completely unrelated to our job function tie us millennials even more tightly to our employers. Money is not the only motivator. We want to see how we effect the whole company picture.
