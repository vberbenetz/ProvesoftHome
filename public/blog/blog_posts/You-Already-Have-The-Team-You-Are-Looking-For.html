You Already Have The Team You Are Looking For

  • June 6, 2015
  • Joe Grande
  •

The Need

The typical customer of 2015 is more demanding than ever and companies need to begin spending more time and effort into satisfying those needs. This generation is less patient, wants to get instant access to information, and are far less brand loyal than traditional customers. With less brand loyalty and higher customer demands organizations need to play the game smarter, and find ways to provide an optimal service or product without blowing their budgets on headcount. The type of innovative power is not something that requires a massive sourcing effort to achieve and too many organization are negligent and ignore the potential they have internally to make this happen. From my personal experience, and the colleagues I have worked with in the past it’s become clear to me that much of the talent needed to innovate can be drawn from employees who are already on the payroll!

Where to Find Them

Time and time again I see brilliant, underutilized employees slaving away trying to keep their head above water. What I am saying about under utility is not active work being performed (because most of the time they are super busy) but actual work that contributes to the overall growth of the company. Now, when looked at from the 50 thousand foot level it’s becomes clear relatively quickly that without those people doing these mundane tasks the business wouldn’t be able to pay their salaries. Customers wouldn’t get product on time, inventory wouldn’t move quite as efficiently and customer service levels would go down etc. So then the argument begins to shape itself into the real important question that all companies must begin to ask themselves,

“How do we get the same amount of “work” done with overall less amount of effort?”

This quote is the basis of everything I believe in when it comes to innovating at the workplace. Top level management needs to buy into a culture of real continuous improvement and put the onus of responsibility for this improvement into the hands of the people who can get it done. I am talking about infantry level go-getters who have a drive to prove they can provide more that what their job description says. The right people for this job don’t need to be convinced, all they need is to be “relieved” or freed up to spend time working on improvements they think are beneficial to the overall organization. Getting these people freed up will be part of my next blog post where I will talk about the importance of “Standard Work”. For those trained in the lean six sigma, you have likely heard this term before. This is one of the key objectives companies should be building an internal culture to achieve.
